### **Stay Safe Online: A Guide for Students in India**

1\.**UPI Safety (PIN Rule):** Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" on apps like PhonePe or Google Pay claiming you've won a prize.



2\.**UPI Safety (Scanner Awareness):** Before scanning a QR code at a local stall, verify the name on the screen matches the shop owner. Never scan a QR code sent over WhatsApp for a "refund."



3\.**Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Enable 2FA on your university email, Instagram, and WhatsApp. Use an authenticator app rather than just SMS if possible.



4\.**Public Wi-Fi Hygiene:** Avoid logging into banking apps or your student portal while connected to free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. Use a mobile hotspot or a VPN.



5\.**Spam Link Skepticism:** Be wary of WhatsApp messages offering "Free Government Laptops" or "Urgent Job Offers" with suspicious bit.ly links.



6\.**Privacy Settings:** Set your social media profiles to "Private" to prevent identity thieves from scraping your personal details (DOB, family names) for security question answers.



7\.**Strong Passwords:** Use a unique passphrase for each site. Instead of Password123, try something like I-Love-Pani-Puri-2026!



8\.**App Permissions:** Regularly check your phone settings and revoke "Microphone" or "Contacts" access for apps that don't need them to function.
